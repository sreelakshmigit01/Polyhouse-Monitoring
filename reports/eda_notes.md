\# EDA Notes



\## Key Findings



\- Temperature shows the strongest positive correlation with yield.

\- Humidity exhibits a moderate positive relationship with yield.

\- CO₂ concentration has a moderate negative relationship with yield.

\- The Temperature vs Yield scatter plot displays the clearest trend among all variables.

\- Most observations are concentrated within a stable operating range.

\- No major outliers were observed.



\## Nonlinearity Assessment



\- No strong nonlinear relationships were detected.

\- The scatter plots appear approximately linear.

\- Linear modeling techniques may therefore provide a reasonable baseline.



\## Modeling Implications



\- Temperature should be retained as a primary predictor.

\- Humidity should be included because it contributes additional information.

\- CO₂ may improve model performance due to its negative relationship with yield.

\- Feature importance should be validated during model training.

\- More advanced models can later be tested against linear approaches.



\## Caveat



Correlation does not imply causation. Environmental variables may be affected by common operational factors such as ventilation settings and climate control practices.
